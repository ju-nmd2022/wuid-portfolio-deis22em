Isa's experience of Jönköping

Jönköping

I want to start off by saying I don't live in Jönköping so I haven’t been able to experience it fully yet. However I do have some memories of it from when I was younger, when my dad used to work at one of the museums and me and my mom would come visit him during weekends. Like I mentioned in the previous assignment, I currently live in Skövde which is about 1 hour away from Jönköping. I’ve also gone to meet friends from uni in the city. Therefore I’ve visited a couple of times and this is my take on Jönköping so far.
  • What can you do here?
    • I remember from my trips to Jönköping with my mom that we used to go to a bathhouse which had a pool with waves. (I’m guessing it is Rosenlundsbadet). That was very fascinating to me as a kid, even though I didn’t really know how to swim, but insisted I would go in there anyways. Making my mom come save me every time. It was fun. Now in my adolescence I mainly visit to hang out with friends or go shopping at A6. Jönköping is also the only city that has an IKEA close by, so whenever you need to go there Jönköping provides. Other than that I like to take walks and there are some nice parks; The City Park, The Town Hall Park, etc.
  • What are some good places to go out?
    • This is a question I cannot yet speak on. However if I were to find a place right now I’d like to try Bar Flamingo, according to Google it looks like they have some nice drinks. And for some themed bar vibes; Dirty Dicks Saloon. As the name suggests it’s a Western Themed restaurant/bar. I feel like it could be a hit or miss.
  • What are some good places to eat?
    • This question I do have a strong opinion on. STUK is an absolute winner. They have so much that is super good. Their pizzas are *chefs kiss* and overall it’s a very cozy café. Another café with very cozy vibes is Frimans. They have quite a big space, nice study area and a great menu.
Isa Dejevik Schröder, Jönköping (2022)
